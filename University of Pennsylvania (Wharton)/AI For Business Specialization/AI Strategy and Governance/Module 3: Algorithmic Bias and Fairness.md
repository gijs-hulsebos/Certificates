### *Algorithmic Bias, Fairness & Ethical Governance*

This module tackles the critical challenges of building trustworthy AI systems. As an AI Automation Engineer, I focus on identifying **Algorithmic Bias**, implementing **Fairness Frameworks**, and navigating the **Transparency-Performance trade-off** to ensure technical systems align with ethical and societal standards.

### 🛠️ Research Methodology: AI-Augmented Strategic Synthesis
All analyses in this module are synthesized using a private **RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation)** environment to bridge business ethics with technical automation.

* **Environment:** Developed a high-context knowledge base in **NotebookLM**, indexing Wharton transcripts, technical papers on algorithmic fairness, and industry audit reports.
* **Process:** This private "Digital Twin" of the curriculum is used to stress-test **agentic workflows (n8n)** against ethical guardrails, ensuring that automated decision-making units do not inadvertently amplify systemic bias.
* **IP Policy:** To respect Wharton's academic copyrights, the raw research environment remains private. Derived strategic assets and intelligence reports are available in this repository.
